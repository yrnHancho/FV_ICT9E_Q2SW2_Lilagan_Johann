Birma The Birman is a long-haired cat that can get fairly big. Typically, men are larger than females. They have large bones and can appear stocky in all of their features. The markings on the Birman are particularly peculiar. She has four white feet (mits), pointed ears, and a pointed cat-like appearance. The Birman's large skull makes the face appear almost rounded, despite the fact that the head is actually triangular. The Birman has medium-sized ears and a Roman nose. The Birman has medium-length, smooth, silky fur. It doesn't typically mat and is simple to maintain.
Siamese Siamese cats are intelligent, sociable, and social creatures that make loyal and loving pets. These kittens, who have been called "dog-like," fit in well with most households.
British Shorthair The British Shorthair is a compact, chunky cat that ranges in size from medium to large. It is known for its attractive features and thick, silky coat.The British Shorthair is a short-coupled, sturdy cat with wide, round eyes and a round face that give it a lovely appearance and loads of personality.
Persian Cat Persian cats are medium-sized, averaging seven to twelve pounds in weight and standing between ten and fifteen inches tall. They feature large eyes, small, rounded ears, and a rounded head. Additionally, they often have plump cheeks and a face that looks pulled in and flat. However, "classic" or "doll-face" Persian cats resemble their ancestors by having more pointed features.
